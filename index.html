Best Time to Visit Kyrgyzstan

Kyrgyzstan boasts stunning landscapes year-round, from lush pastures in summer to snowy peaks in winter. Each season offers unique adventures, cultural experiences, and natural beauty. Here’s a quick guide to exploring Kyrgyzstan’s charm in every season.

Spring
(March - May)

The countryside comes alive with wildflowers, poppy fields, and fresh greenery, perfect for low-elevation hikes and enjoying nature's awakening.

Summer
(June - August)

It's the perfect season for outdoor adventures: trek open mountain trails, visit sunlit alpine lakes like Issyk-Kul, and stay in yurt camps for a nomadic experience.

Autumn
(September - November)

Golden and crimson landscapes, cooler weather, and stunning valleys make it ideal for exploring and photography.

Winter
(December - February)

A snowy wonderland awaits with skiing, snowboarding, hot springs, and peaceful mountain serenity.

8 Days Best Of Kyrgyzstan

Day 1 : Bishkek - Chon Kemin

Drive: 150km

The journey is expected to take around 2 hours. Our guest house in Chon Kemin is nestled next to the mountains, and we anticipate arriving by noon. Lunch will be served at the guest house, providing an opportunity to unwind and appreciate the beauty of the Chon Kemin Valley. After lunch, we'll embrace the valley's beauty with a 2-3 hour horseback ride—a wonderful way to immerse ourselves in the stunning scenery and experience the tranquility of the area. In the evening learn more about kyrgyz traditional clothing: local aunty will come and show you Elechek traditional headdress.

Overnight: Jekshen guest house or Kok Archa guest house

Day 2 : Chon Kemin–Kochkor – Naryn – Shabyr

Drive: 280 km

Early morning drive from Chon Kemin to Central Kyrgyzstan, heading towards Naryn. On the way, we will stop to see traditional Kyrgyz carpet-making, a UNESCO heritage craft. Lunch will be served in Naryn city. From there, it's a two-hours drive to Shabyr yurt camp, followed by a walk through the forest to the camp. Check into the yurts and enjoy dinner at the camp.

Overnight: Shabyr yurt camp

Day 3 : Shabyr – Song Kol

Drive: 50km off road drive

Today, we have a 2.5-hour trekking from the yurt camp to the Moldo Ashuu pass (please prepare trekking or walking shoes), which is one of the untouched places in Central Kyrgyzstan. We'll stop for a picnic lunch next to the Uzun Bulak river. Near Moldo Ashuu pass, our car will pick us up, and we'll head to Song Kol Lake, taking in the beauty of the southern shore. By afternoon at 2 pm we will arrive at our yurt camp, enjoy your time in Song kol. Dinner at the camp.

Overnight: Mirlan or Baiysh yurt camp

Day 4 : Song Kol – Bokonbayevo village

Drive: 250km (100 km off road)

By noon at 11 am we will drive to Bokonbayevo village to explore the Issyk-Kul region, starting with the south shore. On the way, we'll visit Teskey Torpok waterfall and have lunch in Kochkor village. After lunch, an eagle man will demonstrate traditional Kyrgyz eagle hunting with a golden eagle. We'll then relax by the Issyk-Kul Lake—feel free to swim or meditate by the mountain lake. Dinner will be served at the guest house.

Overnight: Emily guest house or Altyn Oimok

Day 5 : Bokonbayevo-Karakol

Drive: 150 km

After breakfast we will drive to Fairy tail canyons to walk between red rocks in the morning lights. By noon we will arrive at Barskoon village for a yurt set up with Meken ava- he is the one the best yurt producers in Kyrgyzstan. Lunch in Meken ava’s house. After lunch we will drive to Jeti oguz( 7 bulls canyon) to see global lift up traces. By evening you will arrive in Karakol city visit Dungan mosque and Orthodox church. Dinner at ethno restaurant.

Overnight: Matsunoki Japanese House

Day 6 : Karakol – Altyn Arashan- Karakol

Drive: 50 km off road

Today we have an adventure to Altyn arashan valley-we call it the land of hot springs. The Russian military car will pick you up at 8 am, drive will take around 2 hours, it's a very stony, bumpy off road drive, please prepare for this adventure. Upon arrival we will have lunch in the mountain house. Afternoon time to hike around the valley ( around 2 hours). By coming to the basecamp, I swim in a hot spring. By evening, will back to Karakol city and stay in the same Japanese house.

Overnight: Matsunoki Japanese House

Day 7 : Karakol – Cholpon ata

Drive: 150 km – driving

Today we will explore the north shore of Issyk-Kul Lake. This side is more westernized, as most Russian people settled here during the Soviet Union era in the 20th century. Afternoon, we will take a cruise with views of the snowy mountains. If you want you can swim in the mountain lake. Chill time in the evening on the beach. Dinner at a local restaurant.

Overnight: Olimp hotel

Day 8 : Cholpon ata - Bishkek

Drive: 270 km

On our last day of the tour, we'll drive from Cholpon Ata to Bishkek. We will have lunch on the way and visit Osh Bazaar to buy local dried fruits and souvenirs. After checking into the hotel, we'll enjoy a farewell dinner at Apa’s house.

Overnight: Olimp hotel
